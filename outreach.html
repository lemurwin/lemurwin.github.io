Outreach Product And Checkout Revision

Improving the Shopping Experience

Introduction

Outreach is an organization dedicated to helping church organizations reach their constituents. They provide postcards, banners, posters, movies, etc. to reach out to congregations.

I worked on this as a 2 week contract to redesign the product pages for Postcards, design a mobile friendly menu, and make the checkout process mobile friendly.

Discovery

Navigating to Outreach.com is an overload, reminicent of mailers from the local grocery store. There are redundancies on the home page and the navigational experience is inconsistent.

Matters become worse once you visit on mobile, as some aspects of the site were developed for mobile screen sizes, but the amount of information remained the same, creating huge menus that were impossible to navigate.

Enormous Menus

Heuristic Analysis

What I discovered from analyzing the website is that there was no standard user flow. When you navigate to postcards vs. banners, for example, you actually reach different looking portals.

Additionally, some items would navigate you to a page that you were expecting, like link claiming "standard postcards" that brings you to a page that lets you create custom postcards.

Another quirk is found in the checkout, where the drop down for a different shipping option takes mor than 3 seconds to generate, and then when you select a different shipping option, it reloads the whole shopping cart, taking another 3 seconds to load, simply to update your cart subtotal.

Heuristic Analysis Details

Information Architecture

To change the menu, I catalogued every subheading and categorized them into larger groups, reducing navigation menu size from 12 top-level categories down to 5, and the footer menu from 16 items to 11.

Checkout User Flow

As mentioned above, the checkout flow is very resource intensive and required reloading the page every time you choose a new shipping option.

A simple workaround for this was to offer flat-rate shipping, reducing time between choosing an item and purchasing it, because a frictionless checkout is key to increase checkout conversion rates.
