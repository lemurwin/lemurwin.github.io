Meetup.com Research

Introduction

The Problem

With the prevalence of the internet in modern society, networking with like-minded individuals has become easier than it has been previously. However, there is still room for improvement. While some networks focus on existing connections and others focus on professional connections, the options for casually meeting new people are limited. Meetup.com has the perfect prerequisites for making new connections, however, its social functions are not commensurate to this ability.

The purpose of this research is to determine if a social function is needed, and to determine to what extent the need is.

Process

Screener Survey

In order to get relevent data, I needed to determine the user base for Meetup.com including age, usage of social networks, including which platforms and how often, and level of education.

I collected and exported the data to a spreadsheet. From there, I determined the users who used Meetup.com versus other platforms and selected interview candidates.

Survey Questions

Interview Strategy

To test the fit and need for this product, I needed to figure out some important motivations for platform use. To do this, I needed to know why a user uses social networks, which networks they use, and if existing platforms had any downfalls.

To find out more, I developed a series of talking points to focus on a user’s process for finding events, as well as the experience the user has when meeting new people. These talking points served as a framework for finding out information, rather than individual questions, with the intention to encourage an organic rapport between interviewer and interviewee.

Review

Survey Results

The results of the survey indicated that Social Networks are an integrated part of society today. Of the twenty-eight surveyed, 100% have been using social networking for over 3 years. Over 75% interviewed were between the ages of 18 and 34, approximately the same amount that used social networks multiple times a day on mobile devices.

Nearly every respondent uses Facebook (89%), while Reddit sits at 64% and Instagram at 60%. Meetup and Twitter were equals at 21%. Of the three users who didn’t use Facebook, 100% did not use any other form of social network.

The data show that meeting people through an event found on social networks is a very real probability.

Survey Results

Interview Results

Across the five interviewees, a few common issues arose. The interviewees in the core demographic of 18-34 reported a difficulty in meeting new people after college as a result of a deficiency in places to congregate.

All interviewees reported using social networks as a way to vet potential candidates for friendship, as well as staying connected to personal relationships, and all reported using Facebook as the primary platform for this function because of its market saturation.

This usage, however, presents the problem of disparity in the intimacy of content allowed between family/close friends and newly met acquaintances. One interviewee went so far as to create a new account in order to erase potentially damaging content from view of professional and other new contacts.

Discussion and Takeaways

Discussion

These findings seem to support the need of a platform that exists in an area between the intimate and professional. Meetup.com partly fulfills this need by promoting social engagement in public spaces, but it lacks the social aspect of Facebook and LinkedIn, so vetting ability is severely hampered.

By improving the social aspect to Meetup, through improved profiles and the ability to "friend" acquaintances, more users will be drawn to Meetup as a way of building connections. Creating a new platform to fill this niche might be too great of an undertaking to address just the vetting problem when other platforms are already addressing the event-attending problem.

Takeaways

  • People use social networks to maintain relationships.
  • Facebook is used mostly for close friends and family.
  • LinkedIn is used for professional contacts and can be intimidating to initiate on.
  • Meetup is not a preferred network for relationship building.
  • There is a need for a casual vetting platform.
  • Meetup could have an improved social function to fill this space.
