Meetup Case Study for Springboard UX Workshop

A Friendly Addition to Meetup.com

The Problem With Meeting on Meetup

In 2017, I enrolled in a 100 hour UX Design intensive training through Springboard.com to improve my research knowledge and design process. My final project was to develop a new product or improve an existing one through research, testing, and design.

The project I chose to undertake was the reimagining of a popular networking platform, Meetup.com, by integrating new social networking functions that would include improving profile page content, the ability to connect with new people within the platform, and the ability to explore new interests based on these new connections.

The idea for this project arose from my own frustrations with networking in a new area. When meeting new people that I wanted to remain in contact with, I would either need to use Meetup's very simple messaging system (which does not grant me any information about the other's interests), or choose another platform with which to maintain a relationship, such as Facebook or LinkedIn, which have their own downsides, Facebook with privacy, and LinkedIn with persistent reminders to invite people to connect.

Project Planning: Research

Project Fit and Competitive Analysis

To discover if the project had viability and interest, I surveyed 30 people distributed throughout existing platforms, including Facebook, Meetup, and Reddit. The findings revealed that many users found Facebook too revealing and familiar to use when connecting with strangers, while LinkedIn felt too formal. This left an opening in the market for a casual profile that can be used to meet new people without revealing much personal information.

Personas, Empathy Maps, and User Flows

In order to keep the goals of the project on course, I developed personas using the survey results combined with individual interviews. This allowed me to ensure that I would not lose sight of the functions that I was intending to implement.

Prototyping and Testing

Using Sketch and Invision, with a little bit of Photoshop, I created a navigable prototype that illustrated basic features of the new social functions. You can view the prototype here.

To test users, I video conferenced with interviewees and had them share their screen so that I could watch their interactions and take notes. More imporantly, I asked them to think aloud and explain why they clicked where they did.
