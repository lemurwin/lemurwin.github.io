WIAD Event Page Redesign

Bringing Everything Together

Introduction

LA DIA is a Los Angeles based design agency working to promote equity through design. We worked on a project for the international World Information Architcture Day event that is hosted every year.

The project was to develop a website that would provide access to all previous years' content while also showing the current year's venue information.

Discovery

Over the course of 6 months, I met remotely with 8 other information architects and designers to breathe life into the project. We held sketch sprints to generate ideas for the homepage and voted on features that we liked, and assigned feature pages to individuals to break up the tasks.

I was assigned talk pages: the pages for displaying specific talks and topics.

Do your homework!

There are many awesome platforms for viewing content online: Youtube, Vimeo, Lynda, and TED, to name a few. I wanted the WIAD experience to focus on education, like Lynda and TED, and incorporate their best features.

Specifically, Lynda has a great scrolling transcript feature where the user can click on paragraphs and the video will jump to match the transcript. I also wanted to integrate a way to view the presentation deck simultaneously with the corresponding video.

I also like how TED has a Speaker subsection that has other talks by the same person, as well as related topics in the sidebar, making it incredibly easy to find related topics or drill down on specific topics.

With these elements in mind, I used Sketch to build some wireframes for the development team to work with.

These wireframes ultimately went to the site developers and were implemented before the project ran out of money, as it seems the case is for non-profit projects.
